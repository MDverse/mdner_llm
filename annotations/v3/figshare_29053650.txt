Mechanism of Bicarbonate-Enhanced Solar Oxidation of Water to Hydrogen Peroxide over BiVO4

Proper coupling of electrode and electrolyte chemistries
can enhance
both activity and selectivity in photoelectrocatalysts, but the mechanisms
for efficient interface engineering are not well understood. This
study examines hydrogen peroxide production via solar-driven water
oxidation on a BiVO4 photoanode in the presence of bicarbonate
ions. Using ab initio calculations, we show that
adsorbed HCO3− species act as reaction centers, promoting
selective H2O2 production. Hybrid functional
first-principles molecular dynamics simulations reveal that surface
hole polarons accelerate H2O2 formation by stabilizing
the transient HCO4− intermediate. In this process, surface-bound HCO3− serves as a hole acceptor, facilitating
reaction kinetics. Our findings offer mechanistic insights into the
activation of electrolytic species by photo-generated charge carriers,
enhancing selectivity for the thermodynamically less favorable product
(H2O2).
