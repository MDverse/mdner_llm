All-atom MD trajectory TOCL:POPC (20%), 50mM KCl

~400-ns MD simulation of a lipid bilayer, which monolayer was composed of 44 POPC (1-Palmitoyl-2-oleoylphosphatidylcholine) molecules and 6 TLCL (tetra-linoeoyl-cardiolipin) molecules. The background electrolyte is KCl; these ions were added in that amount, which imitates 50-mM ionic force in the bulk. The water amount is large (more than 140 water molecules per lipid), allowing it to equilibrate the electric double layer at a relatively low ionic force.
TIP3P water model and CHARMM36 force field were used; the double-deprotonated form of cardiolipin was used (TLCL2). Production simulation was performed with MD integrator (2-fs step, bonds to hydrogen atoms were constrained), Nose-Hoover thermostat (303K, 1-ps time constant) and Parinello-Rahman semi-isotropic barostat (5-ps time constant, water compressibility). PME was used for electrostatics; cut-off radii were set to 1.2 nm. VDW switch radius was set to 1.0 nm.
Resulting trajectories deposited:

COMBINED-50ps.trr [373 ns length, 50-ps step, with velocities]
COMBINED-10ps-100ns.trr [last 100 ns, 10-ps step, with velocities]
COMBINED.edr [373 ns length, 2-ps step]

The additional ZIP archive contains intermediate TPR and MDP files and a force field folder.
